What we stand for

Six core values. Everyone, no exceptions.

They apply from the founder to the newest cleaner, and every decision gets checked against them. Listed in English and Spanish, because our team is Spanish-first.

01

Ownership

Responsabilidad

If we see it, we solve it. No “not my job.” Spotting a problem and reporting it is half the job, closing it is the other half.

02

Integrity

Integridad

Do it right even when nobody checks. An honest “not yet” beats a fake “done,” because we would rather hear a hard truth early than a comfortable one late.

03

Determination

Determinación

“No” is a starting point, not an answer. Vendor booked, part discontinued, guest arriving at four: we still find a way. Encontramos la manera.

04

Team

Equipo

Cleaners, maintenance, VAs: we win together. A five-star review belongs to whoever cleaned the house as much as to whoever booked it.

05

Growth

Crecimiento

Better than yesterday. We don’t repeat errors, we retire them, so every mistake becomes a checklist item that stops it happening twice.

06

Hospitality

Hospitalidad

Success is measured by the guest experience. A guest never sees the turnover, but they feel immediately whether it was done right.

Visual system

Typography and voice.

One sans-serif family across everything: Inter, falling back to Open Sans and system UI. Hierarchy comes from weight and size, never from decorative faces.

Type scale

H1 / 800One standard.
H2 / 700Section heading
H3 / 700Subsection heading
Body / 40017px body copy at 1.5 line height.

Rules: headings 32px and up, weight 700 to 800. Body 16 to 18px at 1.5 line height. Eyebrow labels 13px, weight 700, uppercase, wide tracking. No serif, no decorative faces.

Voice

  • Professional, direct, confident. Clear over clever.
  • We, not I. First-person plural, always.
  • Specific, never generic. Name the guest, the property, the actual ask.
  • Team Spanish-first. No em dashes.

Visual system

Photography.

Our homes are loud on purpose, and the photography should say so. Shoot wide, shoot bright, and show the room doing its job. A guest is choosing a place to bring nine friends, not admiring the paint.

Lead with the amenity. The room that sells the booking goes first.
Show seating counts. Groups are doing the maths.
Outdoor amenities in daylight, water clean and running.
Wide angle, lights on, counters clear.
Beds made to standard. The photo is the promise.

Do

  • Shoot in daylight with every lamp switched on.
  • Go wide enough to show how many people the space holds.
  • Keep the bold paint and murals in frame. That is the brand.
  • Style to checkout standard: beds made, counters clear, towels set.

Don’t

  • No house numbers, street signs, or anything that identifies an address.
  • No guests, staff, or personal belongings in shot.
  • No flash-lit, cluttered, or half-cleaned rooms.
  • No heavy filters. Colour should match what the guest walks into.

Sub-brand exception

The KushKations carve-out.

Everything above is the Cashaw corporate system. KushKations is the one documented exception: it keeps its own consumer identity for guest-facing work, because that brand equity is worth protecting rather than overwriting.

The rule. Cashaw slate navy governs corporate, internal, owner, and portfolio-level material. KushKations forest green governs anything a guest sees. When the two meet, Cashaw frames and KushKations leads.
